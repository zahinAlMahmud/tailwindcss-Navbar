Great question 🚀 Let me give you a **clear overview of Wazuh** 👇 --- ## 🔐 What is Wazuh? Wazuh is an **open-source Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)** tool. It helps organizations **monitor, detect, and respond** to security threats in real time. Think of it as your **security guard + detective** for servers, applications, and networks. --- ## ⚡ Key Features of Wazuh: 1. **Log Data Collection & Analysis** * Collects logs from servers, applications, cloud, and containers. * Detects suspicious patterns in real time. 2. **Threat Detection** * Uses built-in rules and threat intelligence feeds. * Can detect malware, intrusions, and anomalies. 3. **File Integrity Monitoring (FIM)** * Monitors changes in critical system files. * Alerts if someone modifies or tampers with them. 4. **Vulnerability Detection** * Scans systems for outdated software and known vulnerabilities. 5. **Compliance Reporting** * Helps meet standards like **GDPR, PCI DSS, HIPAA, ISO 27001** by generating reports. 6. **Cloud & Container Security** * Works with AWS, Azure, GCP, Docker, and Kubernetes environments. --- ## 🏗️ Wazuh Architecture (Simple View) * **Wazuh Agents** → Installed on endpoints (servers, PCs, containers). * **Wazuh Manager** → Central brain that analyzes data from agents. * **Elastic Stack (ELK)** → Stores and visualizes security data in dashboards. * **Wazuh Dashboard** → Web interface to monitor alerts and incidents. --- ## ✅ Why Use Wazuh? * It’s **free and open-source**. * Competes with expensive SIEM tools like Splunk or QRadar. * Scales well (small businesses → enterprises). * Integrates with other tools (ELK, OSQuery, VirusTotal, Suricata, etc.). --- 👉 In short: **Wazuh = Open-source SIEM + XDR platform** that gives you visibility, detection, and compliance in one package. --- ⚡ Do you want me to also make a **diagram (architecture figure)** to better visualize how Wazuh works?
